Final Year Project

My four years of studying Mathematics and Physics at the University of Warwick were concluded with a research project that studied the theory of Hopfions and other Topological Solitons motivated by recent experiments with chiral nematic liquid crystals.

I provided the first ever theoretical description of non-uniform topological solitons using the novel solid angle approach developed by my supervisor. I also invented the concept of Chiral energy and used it to bound the Hopf charge of these objects, thereby shedding some light on their stability and dynamics.

Here is what my independent examiners had to say:

Bounding the Hopf charge with the chiral and Dirichlet energies was very impressive... Extension to non-uniform topological solitons seem to be new scientific results from the theory side... Significant amount of results that are systematically presented... Overall, the report is of high quality, revealing a good understanding of the topic and sustained effort in developing novel theory. The stability of topological solitons in terms of chiral energy is an interesting result.
